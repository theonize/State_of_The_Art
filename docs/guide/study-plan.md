# Group Study Plan

*State of the Art* is built for conversation. This page offers two ready-made
paths through the book for small groups, classes, and households — a **full
thirteen-session journey** through all thirty-one chapters, and a shorter
**eight-session core track**. Adapt freely: split heavy sessions, linger where
the group is alive, and skip nothing essential.

## How each session works

A simple, repeatable rhythm — about 60–90 minutes:

1. **Open** — pray, and read aloud the chapter's **Declaration** panel(s).
2. **Read** — group members read the chapter(s) beforehand; review the key
   Scriptures together.
3. **Discern** — work through the chapter's **Discern** views; where do you
   agree, and where do faithful Christians differ?
4. **Reflect** — use the chapter's **Reflect** prompts as discussion questions
   (answers stay private on each person's device).
5. **Respond** — close with the **Self-check** questions and a prayer, and let
   each person name one step.

New here first? Start with [**How to Use This Book**](/guide/how-to-use).

## The full journey (13 sessions)

**1 · Begin together**
[The Declaration](/declaration) · [How to Use This Book](/guide/how-to-use).
*Focus: What does the Lord require of us (Micah 6:8)?*

**2 · Foundations**
[The Kingdom of God](/foundations/kingdom) ·
[Made in the Image of God](/foundations/image-of-god).
*Focus: Whose reign do we seek first, and why does every person matter?*

**3 · Human dignity, beginning to end**
[Human Rights & Human Dignity](/issues/human-rights) ·
[The Rights & Protection of Children](/issues/childrens-rights) ·
[Life & Death](/issues/life-and-death).
*Focus: Where is human dignity most under threat around us?*

**4 · Nation, power & the Kingdom**
[Nationalism, Kingdom & Dominion](/issues/nationalism-kingdom-dominion) ·
[Religious Liberty, Persecution & Pluralism](/issues/religious-liberty).
*Focus: When our faith and our political loyalties conflict, which bends?*

**5 · Justice & the vulnerable**
[Advocacy & Public Witness](/issues/advocacy) ·
[Wealth, Poverty & the Common Good](/issues/wealth-and-poverty) ·
[Crime, Prisons & Restorative Justice](/issues/justice-and-prisons).
*Focus: Whose voice is missing from the tables where we have influence?*

**6 · Who is my neighbor?**
[The Neighbor: Race, Migration & Reconciliation](/issues/neighbor) ·
[Disability & the Church](/issues/disability) ·
[Aging, Honor & the Elderly](/issues/aging).
*Focus: Where have we drawn a line around "neighbor" that Jesus would erase?*

**7 · Truth & the Church's integrity**
[Heresy, Orthodoxy & Discernment](/issues/heresy) ·
[Truth, Misinformation & the Digital Age](/issues/truth) ·
[Power, Abuse & Accountability](/issues/power-and-abuse).
*Focus: How do we hold truth and love — and power — without corruption?*

**8 · Creation & culture**
[Creation, Stewardship & Dominion](/issues/creation-care) ·
[Technology, AI & the Human Person](/issues/technology) ·
[Science, Faith & Wonder](/issues/science-and-faith).
*Focus: How is what we make and use forming us — and the world?*

**9 · Body, sex & family**
[Sexuality, Marriage & the Body](/issues/sexuality) ·
[Marriage, Family & Singleness](/issues/marriage-and-family) ·
[Women, Men & Ministry](/issues/women-and-ministry).
*Focus: How do we hold conviction and compassion together on contested ground?*

**10 · Wholeness & healing**
[Suffering, Mental Health & Lament](/issues/suffering) ·
[Addiction, Recovery & Grace](/issues/addiction-and-recovery).
*Focus: Is our community a refuge for the wounded, or a stage for the well?*

**11 · Peace & the ordering of life**
[War, Peace & the Use of Force](/issues/war-and-peace) ·
[Work, Rest & Sabbath](/issues/work-and-rest).
*Focus: Where do we need to make peace, and where do we need to rest?*

**12 · The Church's life & witness**
[The Unity & Division of the Church](/issues/church-unity) ·
[Worship, Formation & the Consumer Church](/issues/worship-and-formation) ·
[Mission & Evangelism in a Post-Christian Age](/issues/mission-and-evangelism) ·
[Beauty, the Arts & Imagination](/issues/beauty-and-arts).
*Focus: What is actually forming us — and what do we offer the world?*

**13 · Hope & sending**
[Death, Grief & Christian Hope](/issues/death-and-hope) · revisit
[The Declaration](/declaration).
*Focus: How does resurrection hope change how we live and die — and what will we
do now?*

## The core track (8 sessions)

1. [The Declaration](/declaration) + Foundations

2. Human dignity: [Human Rights](/issues/human-rights),

3. [Nationalism, Kingdom & Dominion](/issues/nationalism-kingdom-dominion)

5. [Heresy & Discernment](/issues/heresy) +
   [Truth & Misinformation](/issues/truth)
6. [Power, Abuse & Accountability](/issues/power-and-abuse)
7. [Sexuality](/issues/sexuality) +

8. [Worship & Formation](/issues/worship-and-formation) +
   [Mission & Evangelism](/issues/mission-and-evangelism) — and being sent

## For facilitators

- **Lead with humility.** On *Discern* sections, your job is to map the faithful
  views fairly, not to win — model the charity the book commends.
- **Make it safe.** Many chapters (suffering, abuse, addiction, sexuality) touch
  real wounds in the room. Listen more than you teach; never pressure anyone to
  share.
- **Keep Christ central.** Every issue here is finally about seeking first
  [the Kingdom of God](/foundations/kingdom). Return there often.
- **End in prayer and action.** Let each session close by naming one concrete,
  prayerful step.

> **Ready to begin?** Open [**The Declaration**](/declaration), then gather your
> group around session 1.

# 28 · Mission &amp; Evangelism in a Post-Christian Age

<p class="epigraph">"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations…" — Matthew 28:19</p>

The Church exists for mission. It is not a club for the like-minded but a people
**sent** — entrusted with good news too good to keep. Yet "evangelism" has become
an awkward, even embarrassing word for many Christians, freighted with memories
of manipulation, hypocrisy, coercion, and cultural arrogance. In a skeptical,
post-Christian West (even as the faith grows explosively elsewhere), many
believers have quietly concluded it is more polite to say nothing at all.

This chapter recovers a witness worth offering: the gospel as genuine **good
news**, shared in **word and deed**, by **invitation not coercion**, with
gentleness and respect — trusting the Spirit rather than our cleverness or
pressure. It renounces both the manipulative "evangelism" that has wounded so
many and the embarrassed silence that hides the hope within us.

## Declare

<Declaration title="On mission and witness">

We declare that the gospel is **good news for all people**, and that the Church is
**sent** to bear witness to Jesus in word and deed, making disciples of every
nation. We commit to witness by **invitation, not coercion** — with love,
humility, integrity, and respect — trusting the Spirit to do what no technique
can. We renounce manipulation, coercion, triumphalism, and the colonial arrogance
that has marred mission, **and** the faithless silence that never shares the hope
we have been given.

<Scripture passage="Matthew 28:18-20" /> <Scripture passage="1 Peter 3:15-16" />

</Declaration>

## Where we are

In much of the West, Christianity is no longer the cultural default but one option
among many — often a suspected one, associated in the public mind with scandal,
political power-grabbing, and hypocrisy. Many people are "deconstructing" inherited
faith; many more have simply never heard the gospel as anything but background
noise or bad news. Meanwhile, across Africa, Asia, and Latin America, the Church is
growing faster than at any time in history.

Two failures tempt the Western Church. One is to keep doing mission as
**conquest** — pushy, manipulative, fused with cultural or political power — which
rightly repels people. The other is **embarrassed silence** — so chastened by past
failures and present disapproval that we never actually share the hope we carry.
The way forward is a witness that has integrity: lives transformed enough to make
people curious, words offered with gentleness and respect, and deeds of love that
authenticate the message.

## What Scripture says

The risen Jesus sends his people to all nations — mission flows from his
authority, not ours.

<Scripture passage="Matthew 28:18-20" /> <Scripture passage="Acts 1:8" />

We are to be ready to explain our hope — but "with gentleness and respect," never
by force.

<Scripture passage="1 Peter 3:15-16" />

Witness is by deed as well as word: visible good works that point to God, and love
that marks us as Christ's.

<Scripture passage="Matthew 5:16" /> <Scripture passage="John 13:35" />

Paul becomes "all things to all people" to win some, adapting himself in love
without compromising the message.

<Scripture passage="1 Corinthians 9:19-23" />

And it is God who gives the growth; we plant and water, but the increase is his —
which frees us from manipulation and pressure.

<Scripture passage="1 Corinthians 3:6-7" />

## Discern

Christians agree the Church is sent; they emphasize different modes of witness in
a post-Christian world.

<Views title="How should we bear witness today?">
<View label="Proclamation" open>

People cannot believe what they have never heard, so the clear, verbal sharing of
the gospel remains essential and must not be lost. *Caution:* words without a
credible life and love ring hollow.

</View>
<View label="Incarnational presence">

Witness through long, faithful presence — love, service, hospitality, and
friendship that earn the right to be heard in a distrustful age. *Caution:*
"presence" must not become permanent silence that never names the hope.

</View>
<View label="Word and deed together">

Proclamation and demonstration belong together: deeds of love authenticate the
message, and words name the One the deeds point to. *Caution:* hold both; neither
a wordless social program nor a loveless sales pitch is the gospel.

</View>
</Views>

::: tip The witness people can't argue with
In a world weary of religious salesmanship, the most disarming apologetic is a
community whose love is real (<Scripture passage="John 13:35" />). Mission is
less a technique to master than an overflow of people who have actually met grace
— offered, never forced, "with gentleness and respect."
:::

## Self-check

<SelfCheck q="How is faithful evangelism different from manipulation or coercion?">

Faithful witness is **invitation, not pressure**: it shares good news "with
gentleness and respect" (<Scripture passage="1 Peter 3:15-16" />), backs words with
a credible life and love (<Scripture passage="John 13:35" />), and trusts God to
give the growth (<Scripture passage="1 Corinthians 3:6-7" />). Manipulation,
coercion, fear tactics, and fusing the gospel with cultural or political power
betray the message — as does the embarrassed silence that never shares it at all.

</SelfCheck>

<SelfCheck q="Should the Church witness in word or in deed?">

Both — they belong together. Deeds of love and good works make the gospel visible
and credible ("let your light shine before others… that they may glorify your
Father," <Scripture passage="Matthew 5:16" />), while words name the One those
deeds point to, since "faith comes from hearing." A wordless social program and a
loveless sales pitch each fall short; faithful mission is proclamation *and*
demonstration.

</SelfCheck>

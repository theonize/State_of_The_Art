# The Declaration

<p class="epigraph">"He has shown you, O mortal, what is good. And what does the LORD require of you? To act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God." — Micah 6:8</p>

## Preamble

The Church does not exist to win the arguments of its age. It exists to bear
witness to Jesus Christ — crucified, risen, and reigning — and to love God and
neighbor until he comes. Yet every age presses its own questions upon the
people of God, and ours is no exception. Questions about who counts as fully
human. About the protection of children. About nation, power, and the Kingdom
of God. About when to speak and when to listen. About truth, and the line
between conviction and heresy.

This book is one attempt to answer those questions faithfully — **plainly where
the gospel is plain, and humbly where faithful Christians still disagree.** What
follows is not a new creed. It stands beneath the historic confession of the
Church catholic, summarized in the Apostles' and Nicene Creeds, and under the
authority of Holy Scripture. It is a declaration of what we believe these
ancient confessions require of us *now*.

We write as members of one body, across traditions. We invite you to read,
to test everything against Scripture, and to hold fast to what is good.

---

## The Articles

<Declaration article="Article 1" title="The Lordship of Christ">

We confess that **Jesus Christ is Lord** — not only of the Church but of every
nation, government, and power. No president, party, market, or movement holds
the allegiance that belongs to him alone. We renounce every idolatry that would
put another name above the name of Jesus.

<Scripture passage="Philippians 2:9-11" /> <Scripture passage="Colossians 1:15-20" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 2" title="The Word of God">

We receive the Holy Scriptures as the Word of God written, sufficient and
trustworthy for faith and life, read within the community of the faithful and
illumined by the Spirit. We refuse to bend the text to our politics, and we
refuse to silence it where it judges us.

<Scripture passage="2 Timothy 3:16-17" /> <Scripture passage="Psalm 119:105" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 3" title="The Dignity of Every Person">

We declare that **every human being bears the image of God** — born and unborn,
near and far, friend and stranger, the powerful and the powerless, those who
agree with us and those who do not. Human dignity is not earned, granted by the
state, or forfeited by weakness. It is given by God and may not be revoked by
us.

<Scripture passage="Genesis 1:27" /> <Scripture passage="James 3:9" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 4" title="The Priority of the Kingdom">

We seek **first the Kingdom of God and his righteousness.** The Kingdom is not
identical with any nation or empire; it transcends and judges them all. We are
citizens of heaven before we are citizens of any country, and we hold our
earthly loyalties loosely and our heavenly loyalty without compromise.

<Scripture passage="Matthew 6:33" /> <Scripture passage="Philippians 3:20" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 5" title="The Protection of the Vulnerable">

We commit to guard the **least of these** — children, the poor, the disabled,
the abused, the refugee, the unborn, and the dying. We renounce every system,
and every leader, that sacrifices the vulnerable for power, profit, or
reputation. How a community treats its weakest members is the truest measure of
its righteousness before God.

<Scripture passage="Matthew 25:40" /> <Scripture passage="Isaiah 1:17" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 6" title="Justice and Mercy">

We will **act justly, love mercy, and walk humbly** — not as a political program
borrowed from left or right, but as obedience to God. We reject both a justice
without mercy and a mercy that excuses injustice. We will speak for those who
have no voice, and we will examine our own hands before we point at others.

<Scripture passage="Micah 6:8" /> <Scripture passage="Amos 5:24" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 7" title="The Truth, Spoken in Love">

We will **contend for the faith** once delivered to the saints, naming error
where it denies the gospel — and we will do so in love, without slander,
caricature, or cruelty. We refuse to call heresy what is merely disagreement,
and we refuse to call faithful what in fact denies Christ.

<Scripture passage="Ephesians 4:15" /> <Scripture passage="Jude 1:3" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 8" title="The Care of Creation">

We accept the calling to **tend and keep** the earth as stewards under God, not
as owners or destroyers. Dominion is responsibility, not exploitation. We will
not trade the inheritance of generations to come for the comfort or greed of
the present.

<Scripture passage="Genesis 2:15" /> <Scripture passage="Psalm 24:1" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 9" title="Love of Neighbor and Enemy">

We will **love our neighbors as ourselves** and even love our enemies, blessing
those who curse us. We renounce contempt, dehumanization, and violence of speech
and hand. The world will know we belong to Christ by our love — not by our
victories.

<Scripture passage="Matthew 5:44" /> <Scripture passage="John 13:35" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 10" title="Humility in Disagreement">

We hold our convictions with courage and our brothers and sisters with grace.
On the essentials, unity; in the disputable, liberty; in all things, charity. We
confess that we see in part, and we welcome correction from Scripture and from
the wider Church.

<Scripture passage="Romans 14:1-12" /> <Scripture passage="1 Corinthians 13:12" />

</Declaration>

<Declaration article="Article 11" title="Hope">

We do not despair. Christ is risen; the grave is empty; the verdict of history
belongs to God. We labor not to save the world by our own strength but to bear
faithful witness until the King returns to make all things new.

<Scripture passage="Revelation 21:5" /> <Scripture passage="Romans 8:18-25" />

</Declaration>
